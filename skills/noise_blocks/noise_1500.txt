The steering committee responsible for overseeing the enterprise resource planning system upgrade held its sixth progress review meeting last Thursday. The meeting was attended by representatives from finance, operations, human resources, and the external implementation partner. The primary agenda item concerned the delay in completing the data migration from the legacy payroll module, which has been attributed to unexpected inconsistencies in historical records dating back to the period before the previous system consolidation. The project manager confirmed that a revised migration schedule would be presented to the committee within five working days and that the overall go-live date, currently set for the end of the following quarter, remains under review.

Routine testing of the emergency generator systems across all three campus buildings was completed successfully last week. The tests confirmed that all six generators achieved full operational capacity within the prescribed thirty-second switchover window. Two minor discrepancies were noted in the fuel level sensors for generators three and five in the annex building, and work orders have been raised to replace the affected sensors before the next scheduled maintenance cycle. The facilities team has also recommended that the testing frequency be increased from biannual to quarterly in light of the increased reliance on uninterruptible power supply systems following recent expansions to the server room infrastructure.

The joint subcommittee on student welfare and academic support services presented its interim findings to the academic board at last month's plenary session. The report highlighted a statistically significant correlation between participation in peer mentoring programmes during the first semester and improved academic performance outcomes at the end-of-year examinations. The subcommittee has recommended that the voluntary peer mentoring scheme be expanded to all undergraduate programmes with effect from the following academic year, and that dedicated coordinator roles be created within the student services department to manage the increased operational requirements of the expanded programme.

The regional safety officer conducted an unannounced inspection of the workshop and laboratory facilities on the northern precinct last Tuesday. The inspection covered fire exit accessibility, storage of hazardous materials, personal protective equipment availability, and adherence to manual handling guidelines. Overall compliance was rated as satisfactory, with the exception of one storage room in the mechanical workshop where compressed gas cylinders were found to be inadequately secured. The matter was addressed on the same day, and a formal corrective action record has been raised against the workshop supervisor. A follow-up inspection of that specific area will be conducted within thirty days to verify that the corrective measures remain in place.

The annual supplier performance review has concluded for the current financial year. A total of twenty-seven suppliers across the categories of office consumables, professional services, maintenance contractors, and catering were evaluated against the standard criteria of quality, delivery reliability, responsiveness, and value for money. Four suppliers received an overall rating of outstanding, nineteen received a rating of satisfactory, and four were rated as requiring improvement. Formal improvement notices have been issued to the four underperforming suppliers, who will be subject to quarterly performance reviews for the next twelve months. Two of the four underperforming suppliers are currently the only approved vendors in their respective categories, which the procurement team has identified as a supply chain risk requiring prompt mitigation through the identification and qualification of alternative sources.

The environmental sustainability working group published its mid-year progress report against the organisation's five-year sustainability targets. Key highlights include a fourteen percent reduction in energy consumption per square metre of occupied space compared to the baseline year, a thirty-one percent increase in the proportion of waste diverted from landfill through recycling and composting programmes, and the successful commissioning of a rooftop solar installation on the main administrative building that is expected to offset approximately eight percent of that building's annual electricity consumption. Progress against the target for reducing business travel emissions has been slower than anticipated, with the working group noting that the return to in-person conference attendance following the relaxation of remote working guidance has partially reversed the gains made during the preceding two years.

A working group established to review the organisation's intellectual property management policies has completed its consultations with faculty, research support staff, and the technology transfer office. The group's draft report recommends a series of revisions to the current policy framework, including clearer guidelines on the disclosure obligations of researchers who undertake consultancy work, updated provisions for the treatment of intellectual property arising from collaborative projects with external partners, and a streamlined process for filing provisional patent applications in the early stages of commercially promising research programmes. The draft report will be circulated to all relevant stakeholders for comment before being finalised and presented to the governing body at its next scheduled meeting.

The information security team completed the annual review of user access rights across all core enterprise systems last month. The review identified two hundred and thirty-four user accounts that retained access permissions beyond the scope of their current role, and sixty-eight accounts belonging to individuals who had left the organisation that had not been deactivated in a timely manner. All identified issues have been remediated, and the information security team has proposed changes to the joiners, movers, and leavers process to ensure that access provisioning and deprovisioning are managed in a more timely and systematic manner going forward. A revised policy document incorporating the proposed process changes is expected to be ready for management sign-off within three weeks.

The catering services provider has submitted a proposal for the introduction of a new cashless payment system across all staff dining facilities. The proposed system would replace the current combination of cash, card terminals, and pre-loaded meal credit cards with a single unified platform that supports contactless card payments and a mobile application for advance ordering and payment. The estimated capital cost of the transition is included in the proposal, and the operations committee has been asked to evaluate the business case and provide a recommendation to the director of facilities before the end of the current month. Staff feedback gathered through the annual catering satisfaction survey will also be taken into consideration as part of the evaluation process.

The travel and expenses policy has been updated to reflect changes in approved per diem rates for domestic and international travel, revised mileage reimbursement rates for personal vehicle use on organisational business, and updated guidance on the circumstances under which premium economy or business class air travel may be approved. The revised policy takes effect from the first day of the next financial year. Employees who have travel booked beyond that date should review the new rates and approval thresholds before submitting expense claims. The finance department has prepared a summary document highlighting the key changes, which is available on the staff intranet alongside the full revised policy text.
